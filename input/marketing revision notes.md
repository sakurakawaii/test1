## Promotional Messaging — Key Themes

Marketing is building messaging around three core pillars, drafted in consultation with Curatorial's early thematic outline:

1. **Continuity** — how artistic traditions echo across cultures and centuries.
2. **Craftsmanship** — highlighting techniques from bronze casting to oil painting.
3. **Connection** — inviting visitors to see themselves reflected in ancient and modern works alike.

Draft tagline candidates:
- "Where the Past Speaks to the Present" — front-runner
- "Listen to History" — shorter, but Digital Media worried it's too abstract for wayfinding signage
- "Art Never Stops Echoing" — Curatorial felt this leaned too casual for the exhibition's tone

**[PENDING]** Tagline decision deferred until brand review meeting with Communications Director.

## Branding Consistency Notes

- Confirmed exhibition typography will follow the museum's standard serif display font for headers, consistent with prior special exhibitions. Marketing had briefly proposed a custom typeface for a more "ancient inscription" feel, but this was **rejected** by the Design department due to accessibility and legibility concerns for wall text at scale.
- Color palette discussion: Marketing initially suggested a terracotta and gold scheme to evoke antiquity. Exhibitions Design is exploring a broader palette that also accommodates the Renaissance and Modern galleries, so the terracotta/gold scheme may end up restricted to entrance signage only rather than the full exhibition identity.
- Logo lockup for the exhibition (title + museum wordmark) is in second draft. First draft used a laurel wreath icon; Curatorial flagged this as visually overrepresenting the Classical gallery relative to the rest of the show, so the icon is being reconsidered.
