Echoes of Civilization: Art Across Time
Objects, memory, and renewal across five millennia

Opening October 16, 2026, this landmark exhibition brings together more than forty objects from six lending institutions. Visitors move through Origins and Afterlives, Faith and Empire, Crossroads, and Echoes Now before entering a final reflection lounge.

The exhibition is fully accessible, with large-print labels, complete audio transcripts, tactile experiences in every gallery, seating throughout, and a Spanish-language introduction online and in the galleries.

### Departmental Reconciliation Pass 1

This pass was circulated on July 8 and described as **approved** for the use named in the forwarding note. It describes the exhibition as containing five galleries and covering five millennia. The recognition line begins with the Harlan Family Foundation. These details were copied from the working page rather than re-entered from the signed source.

Reviewers were asked to preserve already approved wording, but they were not given a single approved master. Curatorial comments apply to interpretive meaning; Registration comments apply to object facts; Development comments apply to recognition hierarchy; Accessibility comments apply to visitor-facing readability; Production comments apply to what had already been typeset. A mark of approval in one column therefore does not settle the other columns.

The attached comparison retained obsolete language in gray for context. In plain-text exports the gray styling is lost, so current and obsolete passages appear equivalent. Strikethrough was also removed from two lines during export. The resulting record is complete as an archive but unreliable as a clean source of public copy.
