SUPPORT TICKET SUMMARY — EventBridge Migration Issues
Compiled by: Support Engineering
Period: 2026-07-01 to 2026-09-08
Status: Internal reference only, not published

---

This summary covers support tickets related to EventBridge migration. Provided for
Platform Engineering awareness ahead of the December removal date.

---

TICKET CATEGORY: Signature Validation Failures

Volume: 11 tickets
Resolution: Mixed — 8 closed, 3 open

Summary:
Most tickets involved teams validating against X-PiSync-Signature on EventBridge
deliveries. EventBridge deliveries carry X-EventBridge-Signature; X-PiSync-Signature
is not present. Resolved by pointing teams to the v2 spec.

Three tickets remain open. Two involve Risk Alerts Service — reporting intermittent
signature validation failures on risk.alert_raised events even after updating their
header reference. Escalated to Platform Engineering 2026-08-29; no response as of
this summary's compilation date.

Note: The intermittent nature of Risk Alerts failures is consistent with a service
still handling some events via the legacy path.

One open ticket: a team cited an internal document instructing them to use
X-EventBridge-Sig (abbreviated, missing the final 'nature'). Support was unable
to identify the source document. The full correct header name is X-EventBridge-Signature.

TICKET CATEGORY: Subscription Registration Errors

Volume: 9 tickets
Resolution: 7 closed, 2 open

Summary:
Seven tickets: teams attempting POST /webhooks/register, the legacy PiSync endpoint.
EventBridge subscriptions must be registered via POST /subscriptions. Resolved by
directing teams to the correct endpoint.

Two tickets remain open, both involving POST /subscriptions returning 404 in the
staging environment. Three separate teams have now reported this. Platform Engineering
has been notified (2026-09-08). No confirmed explanation as of this summary's
compilation date — Platform Engineering's initial response was that staging 404s are
a staging-environment issue, not a path change, but the issue is unresolved.

One open ticket from the Vantix partner team requesting confirmation of the correct
endpoint — they cited conflicting guidance from "an internal migration guide." Source
of that guide not confirmed.

---

TICKET CATEGORY: DLQ Threshold Confusion

Volume: 8 tickets
Resolution: 3 closed, 5 open

Summary:
Teams are citing three different DLQ thresholds when raising tickets: 3, 4, and 5.
Support has been advising 5 as the working assumption per the v2.0.1 spec, pending
formal gateway confirmation. Several teams have pushed back citing the v2.1 release
notes and the SLA v1.0 document, both of which give 3.

Two tickets reference an email from Priya Nair confirming the DLQ threshold as 3
consecutive failures. Support was unable to locate any such email in the documentation
package provided by Platform Engineering. Teams citing this should verify directly
with Platform Engineering.

One ticket specifically asks whether the threshold counts the initial delivery attempt
or only retries — the answer is not documented in any supplied material. Support has
escalated this question to Platform Engineering alongside the gateway config request.

One open ticket: a team has built consumer logic based on 5 and is now seeing events
reaching the DLQ earlier than they expected. They are trying to determine if the actual
live behavior is 3 total or 4 total (3 retries + initial). This ticket is under active
investigation.

Recommendation: Platform Engineering should publish a single authoritative statement
on the DLQ threshold that explicitly addresses whether the initial delivery attempt is
counted. The current documents use different language ("consecutive failures" vs "retry
attempts") and teams cannot reconcile them.

Volume: 8 tickets
Resolution: 5 closed, 3 open

Summary:

James Okafor's September 3 email, several teams updated their capacity planning to
1200/min from November 1. Support is not in a position to confirm or deny whether
that email represents confirmed policy. Three tickets remain open pending a formal
announcement.
