**DLQ threshold — confirmed: 3**

After the escalation to Tariq and Nadia's configuration extract, this is resolved.
The live gateway enforces a threshold of 3 delivery failures before an event moves to
the Dead Letter Queue. The spec language "5 consecutive failed delivery attempts" was
a documentation error introduced during initial drafting and has been corrected in the
v2.1 release notes and the SLA. Teams should build retry logic and alerting around 3.
The retries-vs-total-attempts question Nadia raised is a naming convention issue in the
config parameter, not a behavioural difference — Tariq confirmed to Lena that the
effective threshold is 3 total delivery attempts.

**Rate limit — confirmed: 1200 events/min permanent from November 1**

The infrastructure capacity review is complete. VP sign-off was received on September 12.
The default EventBridge delivery rate limit will be permanently 1200 events/min effective
November 1, 2026. This supersedes the v2.1 release notes characterisation of 1200 as
a temporary migration-window elevation. Teams designing for 1000/min should revise their
capacity planning. James will update the spec and release notes documentation shortly.

**Partner Notification Feed — Deprecated effective September 15**

After confirming with the data team, Partner Notification Feed is formally reclassified
from Maintenance to Deprecated, effective tomorrow (September 15). Under the migration
policy, Deprecated services are subject to the accelerated sunset date of November 30,
not December 31. The Partner Eng team has been notified separately.

**Analytics Dashboard — must complete by November 15**

Analytics has been flagged as at risk. Given the absence of a confirmed completion date
and the unverified volume concerns James raised, I'm setting a hard internal deadline of
November 15 for Analytics to complete migration. This is earlier than the general
deadline to give us buffer. Tom Becker has been notified.

**LogArchive — exception confirmed for LogArchive and dependent logging services**

The exception granted at the September 10 sync covers LogArchive and any services with
a direct runtime dependency on the LogArchive storage pipeline. These services are
exempt from the December 31 deadline pending completion of the storage backend
migration. I've approved this formally.

**Confirmed complete:** CS Tooling, Billing Sync, Fraud Detection, Payment Gateway
Webhooks. Platform Engineering sign-off issued for all four as of this email.
