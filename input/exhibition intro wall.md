## ECHOES OF CIVILIZATION
### Art Across Time

An object outlives the hands that made it. A clay vessel shaped four thousand years ago still holds the memory of a potter's grip. A carved plaque still carries the weight of a court ceremony no living person witnessed. A length of woven cloth still repeats a pattern first knotted by someone's grandmother's grandmother.

*Echoes of Civilization: Art Across Time* follows these repetitions across more than three thousand years, from ancient Egypt and Greece to the workshops of medieval Europe, the trade routes linking West Africa, the Islamic world, and East Asia, and into the studios of artists working today. The exhibition is organized not by strict chronology but by resonance: an ancient form beside a contemporary answer, a devotional object beside a modern reckoning with faith and power.

Four galleries structure the visit. The Origins Gallery introduces the ancient world through burial goods, sacred figures, and funerary portraiture. The Blackwell Gallery of Sacred and Courtly Arts turns to medieval devotion and African court traditions. The Crossroads Gallery traces the movement of materials, motifs, and ideas along historic trade networks. The Delacroix Gallery of Modern and Contemporary Echoes closes the exhibition with living artists who draw directly on the objects and traditions seen in the galleries before.

Throughout, look for the word "echo" on gallery labels. It marks a deliberate pairing — two objects, centuries apart, that speak to the same human questions: How do we mark a death? How do we hold power? How do we remember?

Civilization does not move only forward. It circles back, borrows, translates, and repeats. This exhibition asks you to listen for those returns.

*Echoes of Civilization: Art Across Time* is organized by the Fairhaven Museum of Art. Presenting sponsorship is provided by the Harlan Family Foundation. Additional support is listed in the donor panel near the Museum Store.
