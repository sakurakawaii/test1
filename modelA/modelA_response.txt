Why — and why the headline number must be corrected first
The W22 slide says quality got worse during the pilot. It didn't — that's a mix artifact. The blended rate rose 4.08 → 4.25 QA rows per 1,000 packs (+4.1%, marked red), but at line level no line worsened materially and the two highest-defect lines improved sharply (FP5 −15.8%, FP6 −12.9%; FP1–FP4 roughly flat). The blend rises because volume shifted toward the high-defect RGD format (19% → 26% of packs; FP5/FP6 weekly output roughly doubled). Re-weighted to the baseline mix, the pilot rate is 3.89/1,000, i.e. −4.8% — the headline reverses. This is conservative, since the baseline predates the cameras entirely (old manual QA-export detection) while the pilot counts camera-driven catches. The "amber" seal/insert rises are also rated against all packs including STD lines that can't produce those defects; on the correct denominator they rise ~9%, consistent with better detection — every such case was held and reworked before route close, and mature customer complaints stayed flat (12 vs 12). The end-to-end trail for the worst case (QP-2556) reconciles cleanly across camera, station, override and QPulse records, so the detect→hold→rework→close mechanism demonstrably works.
