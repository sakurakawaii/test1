# Editorial Compliance Report
## *Echoes of Civilization: Art Across Time* — Fairhaven Museum of Art
**Prepared for:** Publication review meeting
**Review basis:** Exhibition Style Guide, Version 3.2 (approved July 8, 2026), plus owner-approved records identified below
**Scope:** All 12 files in the "Museum Exhibition" package (consolidated working archive, exported July 17, 2026)

---

## 1. Executive Summary and Verdict

**Overall compliance score: 38/100. The package is not safe to treat as publication-ready.**

This is not primarily a writing-quality problem. The prose is generally strong, and large portions of the copy follow the style guide's mechanical conventions well. The problem is **reconciliation**: the public-facing copy in this package has not been brought into line with the owner-approved decision records that the museum's own information-use protocol makes controlling. Two of the exhibition's most sensitive objects — the **Benin plaque (1975.3.9)** and the **Mokoena commission (*Repaired Vessel*)** — carry factually wrong, superseded, or explicitly prohibited content in the label deck, the caption sheet, and the web preview. The donor recognition copy contradicts the Development Office's July 13 release (D-118) in every channel. The web preview build fails nearly every owner check that applies to it.

There **is** a coherent authority chain in this package — Style Guide v3.2, Change Notice 14, the July 10 fabrication release, the July 11 Registration clearance extract, Addendum P-9, Development release D-118, the July 14 Accessibility record, the July 15 rights register, and the July 17 Visitor Experience extract. Read together under the information-use protocol (Rev 4.1), these records agree with one another almost completely. The copy files simply were not updated against them. That is fixable, and the fix path is clear — but nothing visitor- or public-facing in this package should be released, proofed, or quoted until that reconciliation is done.

## 3. Scoring Methodology

Each review target starts at **100**. Deductions apply **only to Confirmed violations** traceable to a governing requirement:

- **Major −10:** a fact contradicting an owner-cleared field; use of superseded or unapproved wording where exact wording is required; publication of explicitly prohibited content (e.g., unassigned accession numbers, access-completion claims); replacement of jointly approved text (P-9); deviation from a Development release.
- **Structural −5:** a missing or misordered required element under §7 in a label.
- **Minor −2:** each mechanical instance — a label outside the §6 word-count range, a spaced em dash parenthetical (§4), a straight-quote passage (§4), a prohibited pronoun (§2), a non-sentence-case headline (§3), a §7-noncompliant caption format.

Repeated violations are counted separately per instance because each instance independently fails the governing requirement. **No deductions** for unresolved conflicts, missing evidence, recommendations, or preferences not documented in the guide. Scores floor at 0. The overall score is the unweighted mean of the six review targets.

## 7. Where the Copy Is Strong
Credit where due — the writing teams have internalized much of the guide:
- **§8 attribution conventions:** consistently correct ("Artist unknown," culture-led attributions, "Court of Benin (present-day Nigeria)," "Akan peoples, Ghana," "Attributed to the Circle of…", "Workshop of…").
- **§9 dates:** "ca." (never "c." or "circa") in public copy; BCE/CE without periods; en dashes in all ranges; lowercase "17th century"; dynastic dates with CE equivalents ("Ming dynasty, Jiajing period (1522–1566)").
- **§10 measurements:** exemplary — fractions not decimals, H. x W. x D. order, inches with cm in parentheses, "Diam." correctly used, H./W. dropped for 2-D works.
- **§11 credit-line forms:** structurally correct throughout.
- **§2 tone:** active voice, no superlatives, no exclamation points, jargon glossed in context (champlevé, kintsugi), correct tense discipline; family panels correctly use "you."
- **Oxford commas:** consistently present.

The mechanics of a compliant exhibition are demonstrably within reach; the failure is in syncing facts and approved wording, not in craft.
