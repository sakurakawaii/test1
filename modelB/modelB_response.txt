The reasoning, briefly
1. The pack's red quality headline is a mix artifact — the camera is not making quality worse. The W22 slide shows QA rows rising 4.08 → 4.25 per 1,000 packs (+4%). But the pilot window ran roughly double the weekly volume on the RGD lines (FP5: 3,000→5,830/wk; FP6: 2,750→5,670/wk), and RGD/EXP formats intrinsically carry ~3–4× the QA-row rate of STD (seal and insert checks that STD doesn't have). At constant baseline per-line rates, the pilot's mix alone would have produced 4.53/1,000. Actual was 4.25 — mix-adjusted, recorded QA rows are ~6% better than baseline, led by FP5 (−16%) and FP6 (−13%), with STD flat and EXP +2%. Caveat stated in the memo: the detection basis changed (manual QA export → camera-fed join) and the route-hold only went live 28/04, mid-window — so I'd claim "no deterioration, indicatively better," not proof of improvement.
2. The amber seal/insert rises are overstated and contained. The slide divides seal/insert rows by all packs including STD (which has neither check); against EXP+RGD packs the rises are +9%, not +24–26%. And all 11 closed seal/insert cases on main routes were held and reworked before route close — including the headline QP-2556 seal case (auto-hold 00:18, pallet table 00:42, reworked and route-cleared 02:10 the same night). That's the system working, not failing.
3. Field outcomes are neutral, not yet positive. Mature customer cases are flat at 12 vs 12 — but 6 of the 12 pilot cases are from builds before the camera's route-hold went live, late builds haven't aged through the field window, and on 10 of 12 cases the "linked QP ref" points at a different pallet entirely (class-level association, not traceability). Two cases genuinely trace to camera-held, reworked pallets and need a rework-effectiveness check.
4. The £820k/£806k benefit figure won't survive challenge. I removed: £70k pallet-audit staffing (the pallet table is still in every route and standard work says no headcount change was raised — that saving hasn't happened), £54k recheck labour (the W17 LS-code change broke its before/after basis), £35k credit reduction (duplicate case families, all on trial lines), and £89k of trial upsides (unsigned maps). Defensible core: £328k + £182k + £48k = £558k.
5. Trial/night branches aren't rollout-ready. FP5X and FP8 have unsigned maps, are in bypass/map-hold, and only ~94% of built packs were camera-checked (the "pack gaps" of 660/990/810 are coverage gaps, not lost product); the FP6 night 48,120 vs 47,310 count still needs its tie-out; and the FP7 label noise is the Northbank supplier batch, not camera performance.
