## 1. Executive Summary

**Overall compliance score: 60 / 100 (59.5 unweighted average of the six scored review targets). Overall readiness verdict: NOT publication-ready.**

The deeper problem is not the score. Style-rule compliance could be repaired in a normal proofing cycle. The disqualifying issue is that **this document set contains two parallel, mutually incompatible descriptions of the exhibition**, both circulating as "current" during the same July 2026 window:

- **Stream A** (style guide §3, intro wall, gallery labels, object captions, curator emails, accessibility review): four donor-named galleries (Origins / Blackwell / Crossroads / Delacroix), a 22-object checklist including a shabti acc. 1955.2.8 (ca. 1250 BCE, Fenwick gift), a Limoges casket acc. 1973.9.1, a Benin plaque dated 17th century credited as a Whitfield estate gift, and a commissioned work *Vessel Memory I* by **Naledi** Mokoena funded by the Harlan Family Foundation.
- **Stream B** (Content Packet R7, Change Notice 14, July 10 fabrication release, July 11 registrar extract, July 15 meeting actions A73, July 16 approval matrix, Caption Sheet C5, Development Release D-118): four thematic gallery headings (Origins and Afterlives / Faith, Exchange, and Power / Crossroads / Continuities), a ~38-object schedule including the Shabti of Djed-Khonsu acc. 1924.18.3 (ca. 945–715 BCE, Blackwell gift), a Byzantine casket acc. 1958.6.3 ("10th century" per R-31), the Benin plaque dated 16th century credited "Museum purchase, 1975," and a commissioned work *Repaired Vessel* by **Thandiwe** Mokoena funded by the Horizon New Art Fund.

Where the two streams touch the **same accession number (1975.3.9, the Benin plaque)**, they state contradictory Registration-owned facts (century, credit line, provenance wording). That is not a naming preference; it is a factual conflict in fields the governance protocol assigns exclusively to Registration, and the July 16 approval matrix instructs all channels to "use Registration-cleared fields only."

Until leadership confirms which stream is the current exhibition, **no file in this set should be treated as publication-ready**, including the ones labeled "Approved master" in the documentation index.

## 3. Scoring Methodology

- Each review target starts at **100**. Deductions apply **only to Confirmed violations** traceable to a governing requirement.
- **−10** Major: publishing a fact or wording that contradicts the content owner's cleared/approved record, a documented prohibition, or superseding change notice (e.g., wrong credit line, unapproved provenance text, prohibited completion claims, invented accession number).
- **−3** Moderate: breach of a documented structural/quantitative requirement or exceeding the documented scope of an approved exception (e.g., §6 word-count bands, §12 exception limits, use of non-channel-approved wording).
- **−1** Minor: mechanical rule breach (§3 case, §4 punctuation, §7 element order), counted **per instance** because each independently violates the rule.
- **No deduction** for Supported-but-Unresolved conflicts, missing evidence, recommendations, or anything not traceable to a documented rule. **A high score therefore does not mean "cleared for publication"** — several files are publication-blocked by unresolved conflicts regardless of score.
